How It Works

From Classroom to Coaching Brief in One Seamless Flow

The system pulls together everything that matters, surfaces what is most important, and puts it in front of coaches before they walk into a session.

📥
Step 1  ·  Gather

Everything Comes Together

  • Classroom recordings are automatically collected so nobody has to download them manually.
  • Observation history syncs from Standards for Success, including prior walkthrough scores and notes.
  • Teacher check-ins give teachers a simple way to share informal data like quiz results or weekly reflections.
  • PD resource library is loaded in the background so relevant strategies are always ready to surface.
→
🧠
Step 2  ·  Analyze

AI Finds What Matters

  • Classroom patterns are identified across student talk time, participation rates, and question quality.
  • Instructional rubrics map automatically to what was observed, with video timestamps anchoring every insight.
  • PD strategies are matched to each teacher's growth areas from the repository.
  • 3 to 4 focus areas are surfaced per teacher, the highest-leverage opportunities for that specific week.
→
🚀
Step 3  ·  Deliver

Coaches Are Ready to Act

  • Coach Briefs arrive with clear focus areas, conversation starters, and direct links to video moments.
  • Teacher Briefs give teachers a self-reflection view so growth continues between sessions.
  • National dashboard gives leaders a consistent view across regions to track coaching fidelity.
  • Role-based access means coaches see only their teachers and leaders see exactly what they need.
Quality Standards

What Great Coaching Looks Like

This rubric describes what coaching excellence looks like at each stage, from getting started all the way to fully thriving with the system.

Dimension Getting Started Building Momentum Accomplished Exemplary
Coaching Clarity
Level 1

Feedback is general with no specific weekly priorities set.
Level 2

One or two areas identified but next steps are vague and untimed.
Level 3

Three to four clear weekly priorities with concrete, observable actions.
Level 4

Priorities come with coaching scripts, video timestamps, and anticipated teacher responses.
Feedback Quality
Level 1

Score-focused with no coaching language or growth pathway.
Level 2

Suggestions included but not tied to rubrics or classroom evidence.
Level 3

Every piece of feedback is grounded in rubric criteria and specific classroom moments.
Level 4

Briefs anticipate objections, offer alternative framings, and include follow-up checkpoints.
PD Connection
Level 1

No professional development resources are surfaced or referenced.
Level 2

PD is mentioned but without direct links or specific modules to act on.
Level 3

One or two targeted PD resources are integrated into the teacher's growth plan.
Level 4

PD is sequenced by priority and mapped to timestamped classroom evidence automatically.
Use of Evidence
Level 1

Coaching relies on memory or general impressions rather than data.
Level 2

Data points referenced but without the context needed to act on them.
Level 3

AI-generated insights like talk-time ratios are used alongside rubric scores.
Level 4

Every focus area is anchored to a timestamped video clip. Teachers reflect on specific moments.
Coach Preparedness
Level 1

Coach enters conversations without a brief or any prep materials.
Level 2

A summary brief exists but lacks conversation guidance or anticipated challenges.
Level 3

Brief includes background, talking points, and guiding questions per focus area.
Level 4

Brief includes teacher history, suggested conversation arc, and clear session exit criteria.
Teacher Ownership
Level 1

Feedback is delivered to teachers with no self-reflection pathway.
Level 2

Teachers receive a summary but are not supported in acting on it independently.
Level 3

Teacher Portal provides focus areas, self-coaching prompts, and linked video moments.
Level 4

Teachers co-construct their own next steps and progress is tracked across coaching cycles.
Regional Consistency
Level 1

No cross-region visibility. Implementation varies significantly by location.
Level 2

Basic aggregation exists but metrics are not standardized across regions.
Level 3

Leaders can compare coaching fidelity across regions on a shared dashboard.
Level 4

Real-time variance alerts flag regions where implementation gaps are emerging before they compound.
Trust in AI Insights
Level 1

Coaches accept or dismiss AI output without any way to flag disagreement.
Level 2

Coaches can flag concerns but there is no feedback loop back into the system.
Level 3

A structured override process exists. Coach edits are logged and reviewed.
Level 4

AI confidence levels are shown per recommendation. Conflicts between AI and teacher data are explained clearly.
Design Discovery

Help Us Build This Right for You

The answers to these questions shape every design decision we make. The more we understand how your team actually works, the better the tool we can build together.

💬

How to use this section

Bring these questions to your team before our next working session. You do not need final answers. Even rough thinking helps us prioritize the right things. Mary and Iman will compile responses and share them by Friday ahead of the review on the 4th.

1
Workflows and Day-to-Day Reality
Walk us through a typical week leading up to a coaching conversation. Where does the process feel like it is working against you?
What currently takes the most time that feels like it should not?
What do you wish you knew before walking into a coaching session?
If you could remove one step from your prep routine, what would it be?
2
Video and Recording Collection
How are classroom recordings currently collected and accessed, and what would fully automated collection change about your workflow?
Who is currently responsible for downloading recordings, and how long does it take?
Are recordings stored in a consistent place or scattered across multiple systems?
Are there privacy or consent considerations we should plan around from day one?
3
Standards for Success Integration
How does your team currently use Standards for Success, and what evaluation data would be most valuable to bring into the coaching system?
Which rubrics in SFS are most directly tied to your coaching priorities?
How often is SFS updated? Daily, weekly, or only after a formal observation?
Are there data points in SFS that coaches rarely use today but probably should?
4
Speed vs. Depth of Analysis
When does coaching data need to reach you to be useful that week, and would you trade speed for deeper analysis?
Is there a point after which data arrives too late to use in that week's coaching?
What day do coaching conversations typically happen in your weekly cadence?
Would a lighter brief within a few hours be more useful than a full analysis within 48?
5
Roles, Access and Permissions
How should the system determine who sees what, and are there edge cases around coaches who cover multiple regions or share teachers?
Are coaches exclusively assigned to specific teachers, or is there overlap?
What should a national leader be able to see that a regional coach cannot?
How should substitute or interim coaches be handled in the permission model?
6
Teacher Self-Reporting
When teachers share informal data like quiz results or lesson notes, how structured does that need to be, and how often should it happen?
Would teachers submit this through a form, a simple text field, or a document upload?
How do you currently handle gaps between what teachers report and what observations show?
Should teachers be able to see how their input influences their coaching brief?
7
Professional Development Recommendations
When you recommend a PD resource to a teacher, what triggers that decision, and how do you think about sequencing resources over time?
Is PD triggered by a rubric score threshold, a repeated pattern, or a specific observed behavior?
Are there existing PD resources the system should connect to automatically from day one?
How do you know when a PD recommendation actually worked?
8
Coach Training and Onboarding
As we bring 200 coaches onto this new system, what would make the difference between a smooth rollout and one that loses momentum?
What do coaches need before they will trust the AI's recommendations enough to use them in a real conversation?
Are there coaches who might push back, and what would their concern likely be?
Should training be self-paced, live-led, or a combination of both?
9
National Consistency and Leadership Oversight
If a national leader wanted to confirm coaching is being implemented consistently across every region, what three signals would you put on their dashboard, and what threshold would trigger a direct conversation?
What does inconsistent implementation look like from where you sit today?
Are there regions where you would expect natural variation, and is that acceptable?
How often should national leaders review the data? Weekly, monthly, or only when something flags?
What is the difference between a coaching system problem and a regional leadership problem, and how should the system help distinguish between the two?
